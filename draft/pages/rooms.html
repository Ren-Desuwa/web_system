Blueprint - CSD

Management Information Systems (MIS)

The Management Information Systems (MIS) is a central facility that houses computer systems and related equipment for the College of Software Development. It provides technical support, maintenance, and administration for the college's computing resources and network infrastructure. The MIS room serves as the technological backbone for the entire department, ensuring that all systems run efficiently for both students and faculty.

BSIS Room (301)

BSIS refers to a Bachelor of Science in Information Systems degree program. This degree focuses on the application of information technology in business settings, preparing graduates for roles in areas like systems analysis, development, and management. It combines computer science, business management, and IT skills to provide a broad understanding of how technology impacts businesses.

BSCS Room (302)

BSCS refers to a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science. It's a four-year undergraduate degree program that focuses on the study of computing concepts, theories, algorithmic foundations, and the application of information and computing solutions. BSCS programs typically prepare students for careers in IT, software development, research, and other areas requiring a strong understanding of computer science.

BSIT Room (303)

BSIT stands for Bachelor of Science in Information Technology. It's a four-year undergraduate degree program that focuses on the application of computer science and technology to solve real-world problems. BSIT programs equip students with the skills and knowledge needed to plan, install, customize, operate, manage, administer, and maintain information technology infrastructure.

EMC Room (304)

The Bachelor of Science in Entertainment and Multimedia Computing (BSEMC). It's a multidisciplinary degree program that combines computer science principles with creative disciplines like game development, animation, and multimedia production. BSEMC graduates are often referred to as "creative technologists" and are skilled in designing multimedia experiences and working with artists and programmers to create digital worlds.
